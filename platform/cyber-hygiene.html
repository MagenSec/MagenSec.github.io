Four dimensions. One AI-powered judgement.

MAGI assesses your organisation continuously across four security dimensions. Each dimension is a distinct lens on your environment — together they form the complete picture that the Cyber Hygiene Score represents.

🛡️
Threat Exposure
How many vulnerabilities are present, how severe they are, and how long they have been unaddressed. MAGI weighs not just severity but real-world exploitability — a CISA KEV-listed vulnerability in active exploitation is treated fundamentally differently from a theoretical one.
📐
Compliance Alignment
How closely your controls and configurations align with CIS and NIST security frameworks. MAGI understands that an org closing gaps steadily is in a fundamentally better position than one with a frozen, large gap list — progress matters, not just the current state.
⏱️
Remediation Velocity
How quickly your team acts on identified vulnerabilities. Based on real patching history across your devices. Organisations that remediate in days rather than months show a fundamentally different risk profile to insurers and MAGI reflects this distinction clearly.
📡
Fleet Coverage
Whether all enrolled devices are actively reporting telemetry. A device that has stopped reporting is a potential blind spot — MAGI cannot see threats on devices that have gone dark, and the score reflects that gap in coverage honestly.

What makes this score different from a formula

Many security tools compute a static score from a fixed formula. The Cyber Hygiene Score is different — MAGI acts as an active participant, not just a calculator.

Context awareness

MAGI understands the difference between a hospital network and a software startup. The same gap count in different industries carries different weight — MAGI adjusts accordingly.

Trend intelligence

A score of 72 that was 60 last month is a fundamentally different signal than 72 that was 84. MAGI tracks trajectory and explains what changed and why — static formulas cannot do this.

Narrative explanation

Every score comes with MAGI's plain-language explanation of what drove it. You always know why your score is what it is and exactly what would change it — no black box.

Insurance-ready framing

MAGI translates your score into the readiness language insurers actually use — not just a number, but a defensible narrative about your controls programme and its direction of travel.

How to move your score

Ask MAGI to identify which of the four dimensions is dragging your score down most, and it will produce a prioritised action plan specific to your environment. In general, these are the highest-leverage moves.

Address CISA KEV-listed vulnerabilities first

Vulnerabilities with confirmed active exploitation carry the most weight in MAGI's threat assessment. Patching a KEV-listed app moves your Threat Exposure dimension immediately and meaningfully.

Close high-priority CIS and NIST gaps

Compliance Alignment improves non-linearly as gaps close. MAGI will show you which controls give the highest compliance lift per unit of effort — focus there rather than the longest list.

Establish a remediation SLA

Remediation Velocity is one of the most differentiating signals in MAGI's assessment. Organisations that remediate consistently within two weeks look substantially different to insurers than those addressing findings sporadically over months.

Keep all enrolled devices reporting

A device that has stopped reporting is a monitoring blind spot MAGI cannot see past. Fleet Coverage degrades when devices go silent — keeping 100% of your enrolled devices actively checking in removes this uncertainty entirely.
